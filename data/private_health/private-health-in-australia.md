# Private health insurance in Australia

Australian private health insurance has two parts: hospital cover and extras cover.

## Hospital cover

Hospital cover is heavily regulated by the government. Every hospital product must be classified into one of four tiers: Gold, Silver, Bronze, or Basic. The government defines 38 clinical categories — things like "heart and vascular system", "bone, joint and muscle", "pregnancy and birth", "dental surgery". Each tier has a mandated minimum set of categories it must include. Gold covers all 38. Basic covers a small subset. Silver and Bronze sit in between. An insurer can add more than the minimum for their tier, but they can't drop below it.

For each product, every clinical category is classified as one of three states: included (fully covered), restricted (limited benefits, usually only as a public patient), or excluded (not covered at all). Two Gold products from different insurers cover the same minimum set of categories, but they may differ on excess, co-payments, gap cover arrangements, and waiting periods.

## Extras cover

Extras cover (dental, optical, physio, chiro, etc.) is not government-standardised in the same way. Insurers define their own services, percentage back, annual limits, and sub-limits. There's no canonical list of extras categories, and every insurer structures their limits differently.
